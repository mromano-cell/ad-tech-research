Weekly Narrative

This week's dominant story is the accelerating collision between generative AI and the advertising value chain, with AI surfacing in nearly a third of the 35 articles analyzed. The conversation has shifted from speculative use cases to hard questions about economics, governance, and discoverability. Publishers are openly accusing AI data brokers of being the new middlemen who keep 100% of the value, while USA Today reports notable Q1 revenue from AI licensing deals even as traffic and programmatic erode. Meanwhile, OpenAI is laying the groundwork for ChatGPT ads in the EU, and "GEO" (generative engine optimization) is being positioned as the new SEO — though marketers are already questioning whether expensive AI visibility tools deliver consistent results. The throughline: AI is no longer a feature layer; it's restructuring who captures value in the funnel.

Trends Across the Stack

In programmatic, Google expanded AI Max as automation shifts upstream, and rival trade bodies have emerged to contend over programmatic's future — a sign of structural fragmentation. Retail media networks appeared in two articles this week (down slightly from prior weeks), with the headline being The Trade Desk selling onsite retail media ads for Dollar General and a broader argument that AI is bringing retail media closer to the point of sale. Among major ad tech companies, Google led with three mentions, followed by The Trade Desk, TikTok, Amazon, and Reddit; notably, TikTok is recreating its ads for billboards via a Vistar DOOH partnership, and X shipped a long-overdue ad platform overhaul.
