Weekly Narrative

The dominant story this week is the rapid operationalization of agentic AI across the marketing stack, with three articles signaling that brands and agencies are moving from experimentation to deployment. Omnicom unveiled an agentic AI tool that automatically tweaks influencer content for brand safety, while Hershey is betting on AI agents to fix a $2 billion marketing blind spot, and Goodway Group partnered with Optable to feed AI agents cleaner first-party data. The publisher side is moving in parallel, with brands now able to deploy their own agents inside publisher chatbots and OpenAI drawing marketers into its ad pilot, largely driven by FOMO. The throughline: agentic AI is no longer a concept deck — it's a procurement line item.

CTV/OTT remained the dominant ad format conversation with 9 article mentions, more than social and video combined, as Meta signaled CTV as its next major ad-growth lever and HP quietly launched a streaming app to enter the ad business. On the retail media side, Walmart Connect led with two mentions, including a notable SMB push via a streaming ad marketplace targeting small businesses — a meaningful broadening of the buyer base beyond enterprise CPG.
